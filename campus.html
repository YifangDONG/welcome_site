    Associations

    Let’s discover some associations and many others on the website Associations it-Sud Paris !



    MiNET is the IT non-profit organization of the two schools.

    Experienced in IT? Beginner? Manager? Engineer? MiNET is here to fulfill your curiosity. It’s one of the most important non-profit organization for the students because it’s here that we manage the network of the students’ rooms, owned by the MAISEL. The MiNET staff registers the students to the network, supervises all the IT systems (servers, switches, wired network and wifi, etc).

    MiNET also solves IT problems of the students which is essential to ensure that students have access to the Internet all the time.

    Other MiNET services : TV over Internet, mailing lists for non-profit organizations, Hosting of students’ & non-profit organizations’ websites and more!

    MiNET: The IT knowledge to serve everyone!

    Come & join us!



    AbsINThe is the place to meet up during the week.

    The World’s Largest Student Driven Organization

    AIESEC is an association which can give you a lot. If you belong to this association you can live an international experience which allows you to become professionalized.

    The mains goals of AIESEC are to create exchanges and develop the skills of its members.

    You can find internships for French students in a foreign country. You can contact companies to try to have contracts to welcome foreign students in France. You can also work in Finance, in Team Management and you can organise events on the campus.

    Finally, you can live a genuine experience with AIESEC, and leave for an internship if you wish.



    The BDA is the association which develops the students’ culture and creativity on the campus!

    The BDA is made up of 15 clubs from singing to painting, photography to magic or dance. This diversity allows students to bloom in the artistic activity of their choice.

    The BDA also organizes a lot of cultural events during the year: concerts, theater plays, karaokes, Burger Quizz, movies and also trips!

    The BDA is the best way to relax after class with its variety of clubs. You can also participate in all the projects during the year : party organisation, cultural trips, business acquaintances…and also “The Art week” and “Le Cabaret”.

    Rits


    Le RITS est la cantine officielle du campus.

    Opening hours:

    Monday to Thursday:
    Lunch from 11:30 a.m to 1:45 p.m.
    Diner from 7:00 p.m. à 9:00 p.m.
    Friday:
    only for lunch from 11:30 a.m to 1:30 p.m.

    The badge system:

    A badge is given to you at your arrival on the campus. It enable you to enter the campus, to have an access to practical work classes in the building B and to pay for the RITS and cafeteria. You can top up your badge with the machine which is placed in the RITS or directly at the cash desk when you pay for your meal.

    The point System:

    The price for the meal is based on a 10 point system which cost 4 euros. Each dish cost a certain number of point. If you take more than 10 points, each additional point cost 0.36 euros.
    You can make your own meal with a starter (between 1 and 4 points), a main dish (between 6 and 9 points), cheese and many other dairy products (between 1 and 3 points) and dessert (between 1 and 3 points).
    The 10 points meal cost 4€ for students. It’s possible to take less than 10 points but you won’t pay less than 4€.
    Beverages are not included in the price of a meal. Water fountains and pitchers are available in the dining hall.
    Enjoy your meal!!

    MAISEL

    The word MAISEL stands for MAISon des ÉLèves. The Maisel is the Housing Office for TELECOM Business School (TEM) and TELECOM SudParis Engineering School (TSP)The “Maisel” is a non profit association which offers housing, both on-campus or within walking distance, to students of both schools.

    Things to know:

    There are 6 buildings in the MAISEL, from U1 to U6:
    The U1, U2 and U4 are the ones located in the main buildings near the class rooms. The U3 is also on the campus but it is a little bit isolated because it is near the sports facilities (beach volley, gymnasium, tennis court…). The U5, which has spacious rooms, is in front of the school, you just have to cross the street. The U6, built in 2005, is on the cathedral place near the mall center Evry 2.
    Except for the U6, students don’t have kitchens in their rooms; they just have a small fridge. However, all the buildings have a well-equipped kitchen (oven, microwave, electric plate). You can also eat at the RITS.
    You can check the MAISEL room prices on this link. Do not forget that when you rent a room, you have to give a returnable deposit (450€ by check, credit card or cash). Moreover, the first month, you have to pay:
    - 10€ for the MAISEL subscription
    - 15€ for bed linen (optional)
    - 80€ for administrative costs

    At your arrival:

    - A Welcom member will welcome you at the Maisel and give you your room keys.
    - Ask Welcom or the Maisel office for bed linen (15€, optional)
    - Ask Welcom if you want a dishes kit (5€, optional)
    - Do the registration at the Maisel office: room number, deposit, date of arrival, date of departure. They will give you a paper to do your room inventory and papers for the CAF (an organisation which helps you to pay your rent).
    - If you’re done with the papers, just put them in the Maisel letter box.

    If you have any problem:

    Technical problems (room, electrical or water issue), please send a mail to entretien-maisel@it-sudparis.eu in FRENCH (ask a Welcom member to translate)
    In case of emergency, you can call 40 71 from any phones on the campus or 01 60 76 40 71 with your cellphones.
